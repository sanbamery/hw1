Stanford: 4 on-campus beverages you should be drinking

1. Italian soda at Bytes

Many times last year, I’d walk into Bytes with half an Italian soda I hadn’t even started to join my friends for a meal. Coffee shops tend to be more expensive and more crowded, and the Italian soda at Bytes is a great alternative. The soda is sweet and fruity, and the baristas are always willing to mix flavors for you. I recommend raspberry Italian soda, but you can also try other flavors like strawberry and peach. The drink is also a great conversation starter — I’ve had multiple people ask me what I was drinking, and I’ve even gotten a few of my friends to order the same when we hang out at Bytes.

Recommendation: Raspberry Italian soda

2. Bubble tea at Pop Tea Bar

Pop Tea Bar is a great spot for bubble tea lovers. Their tapioca bubble tea is fresh, delicious, and not too adventurous—perfect if you’re new to boba. They also serve popcorn chicken and eggrolls in snack-size portions, which are great for a quick bite. Indoor seating is limited, but there’s a small outdoor area that’s nice in warmer weather. Pop Tea Bar isn’t on campus, but it’s easy to get to: take the 1050A bus to California and El Camino, then walk a short distance. It’s about 11 minutes total from the Gates Computer Science Building.

Recommendation: Try the Classic Milk Tea + Tapioca & popcorn chicken

3. Latte at CoHo

I’m not sure what a Vanilla Bean Latte is supposed to taste like, but the one I ordered from CoHo was delicious.

Recommendation: Vanilla Bean Latte
